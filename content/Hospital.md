## JE VEUX TON AMOUR ET JE VEUX TA REVANCHE JE VEUX TON AMOUR / I DON’T WANNA BE FRIENDS.
-GAGA, 2009.

That first full year together we'd already came apart, well before you got to be a grandparent. Which by proxy meant: I was, too. And neither of us had even a picosecond to think about how we really felt about it until it was already real. It had only been a matter of months since scraping your oldest off to Yellowstone and your youngest choked himself out with a ziptie right after Thanksgiving leftovers went into to fridge out in the garage. I'm digressing. Because there's nothing else left to do but digress. If that.

I was on the beach when those first texts came in, taken in a delivery room back in Idaho where you'd been and left but not really ever REALLY left. I knew who was taking the photographs. Who the photographs were for. You did the best you could, I guess, to weave those worlds. As if they could be weaved; everyone knows you don't start weaving with dry grass, and especially never during fire season. Every summer there was one to put out for each of at least three of your children, and, yeah, usually at least one of mine. 

The previous fall, well before you found out about even becoming a grandma in the first place, you were already on medication for what your therapist was calling "situational" anxiety. The situation was simple enough to explain but impossible to actually situate: you would always be in love with him, and I would always be not that him; and when the final push came to the final shoves, my dear, I really + truly couldn't give even the slightest fuck as to how you felt or what it meant that we were already so rotten inside, it was eating through to the outside. 

It was on the beach there at ironically enough Wedding Rock or whatever it was called on the Oregon Coast. I knew all about it before we got there, but once it became crystal clear you didn't want to really do anything other than pretend at it all, that's when I felt the slow sinking of the sand castle we'd built, something someone else surely was supposed to step in and stop us from doing. But no one ever did. One of our exes surely would. Wanted to, even. But no one ever did. 

You sent me that picture and I knew in an instant that it was all over. We almost set it all down right there on the beach and everything. Remember? You were always the one who escalated. 

Always.

As in: EVERY.
          FUCKING.
          TIME.
         
I have pictures of my three, who were always ready in an instant to pack up and go back to whatever was before all that. No matter how terrible, how temporary; the known is always better than the unknown when we're talking about the terrible that we do to each other when we are crushing out one marriage and lighting up another from its still smoldering whatever. I took those pictures because I really did feel it end that day, even if it didn't and wouldn't for several more years. You hated your life in Oregon and had sold everyone--especially me--that you didn't. I had done almost exactly the same thing, but of course. It all felt so obvious and I felt so fucking powerless. It was a long walk back to the parking lot but we'd taken separate cars and I was making it clear we could keep them separate, completely bluffing and terrified you would call it. I'm sure you felt me bluffing, but not nearly as strongly as you were just fucking DYING to call it, but you knew it wouldn't be that easy and you'd already burned too many bridges back in the 208 just to be on the beach in the first place. 

I haven't seen that photo of you two taking your turns with your new granddaughter pretty much since she was born. The first time I saw it, I drowned exactly and forever right and wherever I was standing. I said it was on the beach. It probably was. It might have been the kitchen in the rental house where we'd steam lobsters and crabs and smell up the entire house for the entire time with something everyone thought would be fun and great but, of course, was neither of those things. And never would be. The first time I saw it, stepped full and bare with a tender foot on that piece of glass Bono warned me about, left dead on the beach.

WELL YOU LIED TO ME CAUSE I ASKED YOU TO
BABY, CAN WE STILL BE FRIENDS?

And when we didn't talk that one through and didn't make up and didn't and didn't and just didn't, I bled out. And you just watched me drown.

Years later, when I couldn't sleep for completely different reasons, I found myself admitted to a hospital that wasn't on a birthing floor but in the mishmash of COVID and Hillybilly Xmas victims who'd either shot themselves in the face with a bottle rocket or, even dumber, pointed one at one of Portland's Finest. For 5 full weeks I didn't sleep, terrified if I did that I wouldn't wake up. You never visited--not even in my nightmares, let alone in any dream--and when I finally got released from the hospital, I knew I'd made a fuller recovery when you were no longer anywhere near to put me back under.

## FOCUS? WE DIDN'T EVEN NOTICE / WE AWAKE IN A PLACE WE CAN BARELY RECOGNIZE / YEAH HYPNOSIS.
-NIN, 2017.
------

When I saw that Benelli go up the walkway and through the front door, I had no idea what was going to happen so I rolled the garbage carts past that horrible Jeep with the 4L plates even though it’d come from a 2T residence that very morning, rolled the carts against the side of the house where they belonged next to all the rest of the heaps of garbage on that property in OC, left there on purpose and subtracted from the lease I was paying him, thinking I was putting our dream life on payment plans. The raccoon thing wouldn’t happen for about another full year, maybe two. The shotgun thing though? Instant.

Because when I was done smashing the carts over + over + over again against the side of the house and it didn’t bring that Benelli back down the ramp and then right up against my cheeks, I pushed through that side door and then blind-turned at the theater room, shoving my feet up those stairs and not at all knowing or caring what was waiting at the top of the single flight.

I always told her that if he stepped to me and wanted to actually physically hurt me, he would need to make sure he killed me. Because as soon as I got back to my feet, his world would end.

Funny, that. Naw more than thought so, too.

## THERE'S A SILENCE THAT COMES TO A HOUSE
WHERE NO ONE CAN SLEEP.
-THE KILLERS, 2011.
--------------------

I know how the story ends otherwise, WL. 

I really really do.

If it doesn’t end the way it actually did, it ends even worse than Priest Road. Vogel Way is light years from Priest Road is what I decided in the garage that day, which was to drive away and just keep driving and just keep driving. And to just make sure and stay the hell away from all future Vogel Ways, but of course that won’t ever happen. Unless I’m the good kind of lucky. 

Bitch, please.

It won’t ever happen because I won’t ever not be an orphan. My great great great grandparents are still looking for all of us back in Nauvoo and I want to make clear a lot of us have no idea what the fuck a Nauvoo even is. Me, I’ve lived and read enough to know it’s where Joseph builds the first temple and where that first temple subsequently gets torn down, brick by brick, by the other white settlers in places already settled called New York and Illinois, Pennsylvania and Ohio. You know, places where the railroads were either all going to or coming from and almost always you could never tell the difference and difference didn’t fucking matter anyway because if those trains were stopping for any reason at all, that reason would very shortly pass, and all its cargo steams forward regardless.

Those kids who came to Utah, Erastus Dodge’s kids, they buried their parents and then lived long enough to have The Church bring them to Utah. This is how one part of my family immigrated to the United States, I guess: by leaving it. By passing through. 

And oh my god how excited Annie Clawson completely was not just to be meeting the Bishop when she hopped off the train near Mount Pleasant along with all the rest of the day’s orphan shipment from back east, but OOHHHH MYYYYY GODDDD ACTUALLY LIVING with the Bishop once she for reals got into town, Bishop Seeley’s house the biggest and nicest and safest and cleanest and brightest and happiest house not only within real miles but imaginary ones that encompassed entire new galaxies and universes and things called hopes and dreams and families and all the gravy of life she could never imagine back in Denmark, 8 years old and born in Denmark and orphaned before The Church sponsored and brought her not to the desert but Deseret. 

Word plus a letter = world.

When she arrived in that town they were scratching out of the desert, More, she was welcomed into the Bishop Seeley’s home and raised among his own children, taught English and ordained in the faith. Bishop Seeley would lock in as the first bishop to serve the faithful there. She would not just survive her own insane childhood; she would thrive there and follow Brigham’s edict to spread seeds and sow them and grow them and harvest them and rinse and repeat and but so she was married 4 times and produced a gaggle of children and named one of her boys the most Mormon name she could think of when he was born in 1900, right on the nose as an entirely new century is starting and that name…oh shit..that name..where/what/who was that again?

Oh, right.

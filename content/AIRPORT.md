## YOU'RE DANGEROUS 'COS YOU'RE HONEST.
-U2, 1991.

One of the earliest voice messages she sent me was recorded in Apple Voice Memos. She was driving a Duramax pickup truck hitched to one of those crazy camp trailers where the entire back half if actually a drive-up/in garage for motorcycles and snowmobiles and all the gasoline to fuel them, and to have one of these set-ups in southern Idaho--to have off-road and camping set-ups at all, I should probably say--was much desired and served as social capital that always seems to have buyers and sellers in The 208. If Tom Spanbauer was ever right about anything in his life, and if wagon wheels buried in front yards are any indication of wealth and worth, for sure if you rolled into any gas station at any time of the year needing at least $300 in available credit just to get the damn gas pumps turned on in the first place, well...you get the snowdrift, so to speak.

She had such a trailer and I saw it the first time I ever saw my future step-daughter, K., then aged almost-13 (?) and unloading or loading the trailer that'd I soon enough learn proper terminology to be a "Toy Hauler," or, at least, that's the phrase she used all the time instead of "TRAILER" because when she'd first got together with the dude, he lived in a single-wide within walking distance of a real cowboy bar just off 2nd Ave in TF, where all the fake cowboys like him trolled for fresh fish and she said would wait all night in his single wide until he came back smelling all the ways she said she never liked him smelling when he went out without her and he hadn't knocked her up with K. 

Yet.

K's mother, father, half-brother and two younger full siblings would cram into the toy hauler for occasional overnights near Diamondfield Jack's in The South Hills or way further east and north, up near Challis and Craters, nearer to Mackay, where "Spar" and "Naw" would crush entire cases of silver-canned beer by noon and pretend they were still capable of swinging their hammers or punching their nailguns with any accuracy past 12:01. "Naw" never had to negotiate anything away or convince her too much to emblazon the family's name on the trailer's tailgate, something with the word "MADNESS" or "MANIA" or something like that. 

Why this matters at all, if it does at all, is because a picture of the toy hauler was set as her profile picture when I Facebook-friended her, and also because it supposedly became her "room" when she'd sell me on the fiction that her and "Naw" were most definitely already well beyond separated + but also, HOW DARE YOU.

APRIL IS THE CRUELEST MONTH. If I could take one thing back, it be that fucking friend request that started and ended it all.

"Happy birthday! Long time no talk but I hope you're having a good one!"

I'd already seen Candy B post something similar to her profile; Candy was my age and went to college in Moscow that same fall me and Jonny V and Frankie and Scotty K all started our freshmen year together but Scotty didn't live through it and then Frankie transferred and then first Candy and then Jonny both dropped out. It was barely 2009 and I was who I was then but now I'm who I am now and but so I went into the flow of that giant river of social media, that, if you stay too long slowly but surely turns more into an ice floe than any delta at any place on earth. 

I'm rarely at a loss or lack for words, but here's that old cliche I'm not supposed to use but goddammit it's completely the truth when I tell you right here: it's IMPOSSIBLE to describe what happened to me when she responded. Because it was nearly instant. 

Lightning strike. 

Her attention, when she focused it upon me, always was a truly indescribable kind of tractor beam; surely I will never understand what or why there's ANY energy there, let alone SO VERY MUCH. Almost all of it bad. I didn't find this out, either; this wasn't a Scooby-Doo mystery of any sort; when you are beyond the starving, in that place so dark and so cold, where the wind just swirls and swirls and swirls, is where the world finally drags you down.

For months, it was almost automatic--send a message, get a message. Send a message, get a message. Yahoo!Messenger was the client and my then-wife even then-worked for the then-company that then-made it and it was already a mainstay in my completely codependent marriage and my then-wife and I even spent a Blue Valentine-style "last gasp"/work weekend of hers in Burbank over a Labor Day weekend, initializing our long and painful and permanently scarring separation and divorce that was so much worse than it needed to be and but of course completely fueled by the affair. Looking back, it looks and feels so deliberate, which of course sounds nefarious as fuck because IT IS nefarious as fuck. 

But it also *wasn't*, in the sense that I didn't ever think past the night at The Place and, of course, I'd stopped thinking entirely before I killed the engine on the 4th floor of the parking garage at PDX so very very late one night that it became the next day way faster than I'd ever imagine a next day could arrive in the first place. 

I was tired. And starving. And she more than filled my plate.

But she certainly never let me rest; that night I didn't sleep at all. She dozed off between and the sheets were pooling and I was fully aware--painfully so, even--that I was fucking someone's mother. Daughter. Wife. Because she'd kept that necklace on the entire time and only the necklace. Through all of it. And when she left, I hid her boots and the anklet she'd worn that had little silver bells on it that dinged when she strutted or wrapped her knee behind my head and moaned those deep moans she moaned. She'd recorded recordings of herself---audio only--of her supposedly stealing away while everyone else she lived with was barbecuing or circling the pool they'd surrender the entire lawn out back to every May when school got out, just before Memorial Day. I couldn't believe the things she'd text or email, and I don't remember at which point the things started coming from second and third phone numbers that weren't the first one she'd defiantly emailed me when our emails started going stale, which--yes--was also pretty much instantly. 

Lightning strike.

She'd send things when she knew for sure I was in close enough proximity to get caught with them, and the sheer trouble of it alone made it impossible for me not to engage and escalate; the digital breadcrumbs alone were ample enough grounds for my then-wife to pack up and leave my ass and kill me forever with alimony and child support. I should have been caught. I tried, even, at least subconsciously. And so did she, albeit not nearly as subconsciously.

Because there WAS a moment I was caught red-handed when "Naw" found something I'd written and given her that was for her eyes only, typed and printed and deleted and everything, a true 1 of 1, and the secret meant so very much to her that she almost certainly deliberately staged that thing somewhere Naw couldn't miss finding it. Like pinned between the visor and overhead interior, both of which were smoky grey-black and the leaves of paper bleach-white. The shit she pulled at that Fred Meyer over Labor Day that first year both thrilled and terrified me, and I knew what I was doing would cost me at least my marriage, if not more. These were real discussions, even: actual, active ones. Consenting adults talking themselves through the talk of being consenting adults. 

And I think what terrified me the most wasn't actual terror, strangely enough. It wasn't terror at all, really; it was high grade apathy and I simply didn't fucking care anymore. 

And it wasn't because SHE came along, by the way; if anything, it was ME who was the invader, the terrorist.

## THEY SAY THAT HATE HAS BEEN SENT SO LET LOOSE THE TALK OF LOVE.
-BEIBER, 2010.
-----

It was the trains that brought them all, really. Way after the conestoga wagons and mule trains and constant hike-walking. It was the trains that picked them up one place and simply dropped them off another. Take an 8 am stroll along Main Street in a place like Canby, OR and you might even be able to imagine it, walking those first full steps in your own legs and your own feet, not so much taking you anywhere as pulling you there, literal inches at a time, into the next whatever your life would or could be. A lot of the people who took the trains out west to places like Canby in OR and Boise in ID were the same kind of people who'd always had to catch rides places instead of being able to drive themselves. In other words, WORKING people. 

Look, I have a lot of bitches against a place called Idaho, and I fucking dare anyone to step into a real conversation with me about it who's intent on selling me something that could never ever exist for someone like me. 

And what do I mean, SOMEONE LIKE ME?

That's simple. An orphan.

That's a heavy word to use for someone whose parents aren't only still alive but still married to each other, at that. Like they were the same things, I suppose, marriage and death; that seems much more honest than equating marriage and life, I've always thought. 

Or at least that's what I tell myself now that it's December of 2025 and all fall of this year I've been feeling the flashbacks of 2010, and not so much revisiting or even questioning as letting myself feel some feelings I haven't allowed myself to feel, namely that daydreamy haze of a love affair in fall months when the leaves are rattling before turning and ultimately falling and becoming what the end of a terrible time always feels like: cold and mushy at best, like the swollen banks of a thin young river that only started finding its way within the last 50-60 years because of some dams way way way upstream from anywhere near. Driving the backroads through eastern Multnomah and Clackamas counties just a matter of weeks ago now, brilliant layers of light and color and heat and wind and but also the emptiness all around me as I drove always by myself and always with no particular place to go. 

When I got to Springville Road and saw the semis and trailers all rounded up and ready for the big November shipments of Christmas trees with all points east, the crazy reality is the same trains that brought working people all the way out to places like ID, OR, WA, UT, WY, MT, NV, CA, AZ, NM, CO, those same trains carried trees back east in the exact opposite direction. 

A fair trade. But super unclear for whom.

If you're an orphan and you're on a train, what difference does it really ever make where it stops and pries you off?

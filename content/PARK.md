## WORDS ARE VERY UNNECESSARY / THEY CAN ONLY DO HARM.
-DEPECHE MODE, 1990.

We met down in the canyon with the Snake boiling just underneath us. It was the first time we’d ever been there at the same time. We took separate vehicles. We’d broken up. It was what you wanted; I didn’t want to give it to you; I convinced you to come back; you convinced me to finally leave. I made so many promises down there that day I knew I’d never keep. The only thing I ever wanted to keep was you. But you weren’t mine. And I most certainly wasn’t yours. 

A few months later we were back and you had a fat diamond on your finger and your kids were on all the other fingers. We took engagement pictures with them and your oldest ruined all the shots on purpose with his acting up; the irony is, he was always the photogenic. A bit of a ringer for Zac Efron, even; he totally has your eyes.

There’s a picture just the two of us that I haven’t seen in almost a decade now, but you are smiling that same sultry smile for the camera that could get me to do anything in the world you ever wanted. So, yeah. I stopped looking at it long before we split up. 

Oh, yeah. 

We took separate vehicles. There and back. We’ll always be in separate vehicles. That’s what I guess I decided that last terrible morning in Damascus. You’ll always see it as a decision that was entirely mine to make, which is why you always never revisit any of it and have already forgotten at least 75% of it.

Me, I’m still working on my own percentages as far as that shit’s concerned.

When I wiped my old iPhone that fall you blew back to TF and dug back in there like a terrible mole or gopher or something, I had every single text exchange we ever had still on it when I touched the big red oval at the bottom of the screen and made it all disappear. When the screen resolve and didn’t fill back in, neither did I. That’s not because all of me was in those messages, any more than any part of you was in them, neither. 

It’s because of the thing I always said to you. That talk is the cheapest form of currency in the entire world we have. 

Until it isn’t.

When talk costs you more than you think it could or should, though, that’s different. That’s when you’re in the realm of I Got More Than I Bargained For, which, in this stupid country, is a literal manifest destiny. But in Idaho? It’s literally written into the flags they wave to make themselves feel better, which is the only thing a flag can really ever do: ESTO PERPETUA. 

ESTO your own PERPETUAs, all you fucking bitches. 

But let’s keep on track here. For once. And hopefully forever. I can’t be lost in the wilderness that long because human lives aren’t that long in the first place.

The last text exchanges we ever had were between entirely new numbers on entirely new devices. When your texts started arrived from a 208 number and your emails from a new address, I made sure to point these things out to you. I was still reeling from the last time I’ll ever hear your voice, the single phone call the court made us have with each other. I don’t think I’ll ever unhear what you said; I could tell you’d practiced it, even. No, you didn’t give me cancer, WL. At least not the kind that I could so easily cut out of my body and move on.

But before all that, it was the spring before. It’s still 2019 and this is the world that existed before COVID brought out all the worst in all of us. But we never needed a global pandemic for that. Bitch, please.

What I realize now is she was making herself sign the divorce decree when she sent the text she sent about how 1) she never wanted a divorce + 2) her granting me one and suffering all of the accompanying whatever would never soothe the rage of the little boy who’d been literally raped and pillaged by his own family for his entire childhood. Both things are true. This isn’t about what’s true; spite is rarely about the truth. Spite is its own deal entirely, viral almost. But less a virus and so much more a cancer. 

WL, you pretended to work in health care just long enough for people who do that work for real got smart and pushed you the fuck out before you injected your bleach into somebody who would actually do something about it. You took over an active Medicare scam your old boss trained you how to run and modeled for you when exactly to jump off the train when the IRS and Idaho Tax Commission come literally knocking on whatever doors you’ve passed through, even all the way out in Oregon City at the literal end of the fucking trail, all the way down to the tackboards to completely mix all of these metaphors.

And but no, at least you are correct about one thing: nothing will ever soothe that little boy. I know him better than anyone because I’ve known him my entire life and he’ll be with me for is duration. We had lots of conversations in the hospital when no one else came through the door and especially when the few people who did left and went back to doing something else more important. I felt like that the entire time we were “together,” by the way. All that tension, all that anxiety. That’s why I felt what I felt when that big red oval disappeared and I knew I couldn’t take it back. 

Even if I wanted to.

## LET’S CONSPIRE TO IGNITE ALL THE SOULS THAT WOULD DIE JUST TO FEEL ALIVE.
-MUSE, 2006.
--------

It’s August of 2025 and yesterday I emailed a reporter who interviewed Earl and Emma for The Times-News in September 2001. I tripped across the article in my research for their marriage and divorce records; Idaho official documents for the longest time have been incredibly/intentionally vague in requirements and particulars. There’s a mining death report form I uncovered at the Idaho State Historical Archive’s website, where the form asks the person filling it out to not just describe the death in words but also to draw a picture of it as well. The impersonal nature of both the form and any information written or typed or drawn or whatevered onto it is hard not to notice if you take an actual moment to actually imagine for yourself what exactly they are trying to describe for you in both pictures and words, neither of which could ever ever come even remotely close to getting anything more than just the facts, ma’am. The bare necessities. The least information required as by law. Avoidance as normative; only tell them what you have to or what you need to in that moment to make that moment become a different moment.

Really, though: WHATEVER you have to do, just do it. Earl and Emma were Depression Era kids living their entire lives with untreated mental illnesses that only BEGIN with the word “depression.” It is impossible that Emma wasn’t a life-long, highly functional yet undereducated BY HER OWN DESCRIPTIONS BY THE WAY manic depressive, and that is just for starters. That’s her at 9 years old on New Years Eve, distracted by whatever manufactured “WELCOME 1935!!” Party or whatever you’d call it when the clock struck midnight at the end of that horrible, horrible day on Priest Road for not just Emma but any and everyone who had to bother to take that right off the highway to even get back where a fucking road like Priest Road would be in the fucking first place. Google won’t send its auto piloting map Jetson-car things or whatever drone things or whatever the fuck they are using now besides just the satellites spinning above the planet to map every fucking square millimeter of the earth’s surface. 9 year old Emma, you will always have a place in my heart for what you dared to try and tell me that Friday night about what actually happened, but I was also about the same age and so maybe you told the white lie to the white kid who wouldn’t ever take the answers you gave as enough explanation and just be done with the question and move the fuck on.

Nope. Sorry, Emma. That’s the Haffner in me. And I am, if nothing else, a Prussian at heart. Oh believe. 

The article is a front-page, A1 not in quality but in J-SCHOOL LINGO LIKE BRIEN WHAT’S HIS FACE BARNETT LIKED TO ALWAYS SAY AT THE IDAHO STATESMAN WHERE I WORKED PART-TIME BY THE FUCKING WAY AND HELPED THEM BIRTH ONLINE NEWS BY THE FUCKING WAY AS LITERALLY A ONE-MAN OPERATION, WORKING WELL INTO THE DAWN WHILE THE REST YA’LL SLEPT AND THEN TREATED ME LIKE THE VAMPIRE AND BUT I GET SIDETRACKED.

So I’ll start over.

Mark Heinz is an old-school journalist who has worked in a bunch of places throughout the intermountain West, including Twin Falls, ID at a time when I was really leaving my family forever and it was already obviously the case. Mark interviewed my grandparents at 119 Clinton Dr. There’s a photo of them leaning against the chainlink fence that had been in the same place for nearly my entire childhood; I can remember Earl rolling onto the lawn the huge spindle of chainlink that formed the fence because MFUP and KLD’s own father had been helping Earl dig the holes for his fenceposts. It’s the mid or late 1970s, I don’t remember. Anyway, the photo is staged to show how the property abuts a industrial parcel of Twin Falls on the south side of town that used to be the actual outskirts and beyond the official town boundaries when they moved there in 1962 and everything around them was getting subdivided and less and less people were doing actual farm work. Earl himself had largely given up farm work, even if he did spend parts of his summers and falls working harvests and keeping his options open. But the truth of the matter is probably closer to something like this:

It’s 1942 and Earl and Emma get married because Emma wants to get married and Earl for whatever reasons can’t tell this crazy Indian bitch no already and will struggle with that the rest of his earthly life. The relationship he begins with her in the early 1940s is complicated to say the very least. They are married on Christmas Eve; Emma is already beyond sick of her new stepmother, Lucille, a divorcee and seemingly old girlfriend of her dad’s who literally takes the train out to Idaho to marry Ben and help him get the rest of his kids through to their teens. It’s important for me to say that Emma’s childhood wasn’t just torn up by what happened that awful morning, NYE 1934, when Clora for whatever reasons decided she really couldn’t and wouldn’t live in their life there a single second longer; however, it’s even more important for me to say this: Clora’s suicide is by far the most significant thing that ever happened in my extended family, and the one thing above all other things that has the most impact upon me and everyone I have ever been in any kind of close relationship with. The Teton Dam footage captures what happens when a dam breaks and the first major surge of water rolls through; the damage here is completely catastrophic; but as the dam empties and the river’s channel widens before emptying completely upon the Snake River Plain, up and out of the riverbeds comes all of the shit someone else thought was completely gone, dead and buried; but because the soil isn’t deep enough or firm enough, it can’t hold all the things buried in it. David Andersen told me about finding milkjugs on his grandfather’s farm that were full of gunpowder, washed there and kept dry when all that water and mud and garbage and corpses and you name it all passed through and left 3/4 of Rexburg and all of Sugar City completely underwater. What if there was more water than that? And it never ever stopped flowing at that volume? 

Clora’s suicide, for Emma, was the Teton Dam giving way and she’s that dude in the earthmover/bulldozer who is trying to firm up the structure right as it’s giving way. Up to the very end, she is hoping the whole thing isn’t giving way; she can see it coming, is what I’m saying. She’s 9 years old and death is already familiar to Emma is what I’m saying. Death is even natural and acceptable. 

Clora’s death is neither of those things. Emma is “lucky” to run away that day, in that Clora didn’t decide to take any of her family with her. That possibility was instantly real to Emma; she could have died that day along with or even instead of her own mother and even because of; read that last chunk back and you’ll see why it’s PREPOSITIONS that matter the most when ya’ll are beefing about which pronouns to use; part of the reason for pronouns in the first place, by the way, is to avoid saying the same thing over and over and over and over and over and over and over and over and over and and and you get it over and over.

Ruele for sure would have seen Emma’s mommy and daddy issues; she also would have been inherently suspicious because the family just wasn’t from there in the first place; I have no way of knowing how tolerant or vigilant or “proper” my great grandparents were I/r/t their faith; Oscar’s obituary describes him as a pretty straightforward Saint as far as public appearances went. Earl’s father was indeed sealed to his mother in a Mormon temple, which made Earl’s faith ordained within their holy covenant as far as the LDS Church was concerned. I won’t get into the weeds here about Mormon ceremonialism, but let’s just repeat what I said earlier: I’m a Prussian at heart; what’s more, MMAB make sure I was baptized into her church, and because both sides of MMAB’s families are 100% German, I too was baptized a Lutheran and blessed as such as a baby. I have never practiced this faith nor do I claim it in any way other than cultural; because of where I grew up, though, I’m more culturally Mormon. Lutheran churches are everywhere in the intermountain West, just like Baptist churches are and even Catholic ones, too. It took me moving to the midwest in the mid 1990s for graduate school to really understand just how pervasive and powerful an influence the LDS Church has on white kids in particular who grow up in the intermountain West; when I was in grade school, The Donny and Marie Show was my favorite show and I was certainly encouraged to watch it; MMAB watched it with me and we would sing their songs and even pretend we were them when it was just the three of us up at the cabin and it’s a Saturday night and the hard alcohol is out on the big counter now and it’s still just the fireplace and the stereo and they just let the carousel fire light the entire space; those are my favorite memories of not just Featherville and not just childhood but of my entire life. What seems to be, you know, it’s always better than nothing.

Than nothing at all.

## SOMETHING BETTER THAN NOTHING / SOMETHING BETTER THAN NOTHING, IT’S GIVING UP / WE ALL NEED TO DO SOMETHING / TRY KEEP THE TRUTH FROM SHOWING UP.
-THE WHITE STRIPES, 2005.
------

It’s significant Emma took me and made me look at Ruele that day in Carey. It was important to her that I actually see not just a dead person; it was important to her, I’m quite sure of it, that I see THIS PARTICULAR dead person, let there be any question left as to who the real matriarch was in my life. MMAB would never have made me do something like that, I don’t think; MMAB has puppy paws and puppy teeth but a Doberman mouth, and I think that’s largely true of her entire family. Yeah, I’m predominantly a Prussian. A Haffner. 

But not entirely.

MFUP and I have separately swam the floodplains and scraped away layers upon layers of mud; I’m way further downstream than him; KLD, too; that isn’t because I’m the youngest; it’s literally because I have traveled the farthest and even backtracked just to make completely sure before I stepped the fuck off forever.

Goodbye, 208. It was never meant to be. None of it. 

And you knew it. Still you persisted. ESTO PERPETUA.

Not this Cherokee.

Not this Prussian.

Not any Shoshone.

Sure as fucking hell no Bannock neither.

None of our languages are Latinate. Tom Spanbauer’s neither. He and I disagreed on some things, sure, but we agreed on a hell of a lot more. Tom posed for a single photo with me the last time I saw him, at his house there in NE Portland, only not in the basement. At his dining table. It was the first and only time I sat there with him. I recorded the whole conversation for a podcast I was doing at the time, which was always just an excuse to talk to people who would actually agree and want to talk to me because I became who I became, not because of Who I Was. The distinction completely matters.

## DEAR NAW, FROM THE DISTANT FUTURE.

Once upon a time I wrote a letter that I enjoyed writing very much. It was all preachy and shitty and your ex-wife and I were dragging you for no better reason than she was obviously still in love with you and just using me to maximum capacity to pay you back for not feeling the same way or whatever bullshit that of course was never and is never and never will be even remotely true for even a second. I have a lot to apologize for when it comes to her, though, and my bet is you might too. I couldn’t have been the first dude she rolled around with simply to make you jealous and to watch you squirm. She had detachable feelings in the places where you sometimes wish they would just stay detached.

I’ll just start over, I’m sorry. I owe you at least one apology, I’m sure. So here’s the one to start: *I’m sorry I slapped your knee at the wedding when they made us sit together like that.* I don’t know between us who hated it more, but as usual I’m going to put myself first and claim that top spot. Dude! I just couldn’t fucking believe it when she asked + knew it was coming + knew it was coming. For weeks I knew because you know how that kid is and how he loves to play people off against each other, especially ones he ain’t blood related, which of course was/is MOST YA’LL IN THE FUCKING FIRST PLACE but as usual, I digress.

I knew that ask was coming because it was overdue. She decided for the occasion you and me needed to have a functional relationship and even now, way way way into the future now, I still have zero clue why. You have to know beyond any shadow of a doubt that I never wanted to fuck with your family, and it really was your wife who convinced me to want to fuck just her in the first place and I was more than a little mystified as to why it continued past whatever she was mad at you over Labor Day that year but HOLY SHIT DUDE WAS SHE MAD AT YOU THAT WHOLE SUMMER for whatever you did, BELIEVE. She had a rotation of at least three dudes that I know of that fall, bro: you, me, and the neighbor across the street that she said ya’ll used to flirt and swing with and I’m guessing she probably still gets together and fucks that one a few times a year, yeah? She swore to me for the entire time we were on speaking terms that you didn’t know about the back and forth across El Monte Street and I always called bullshit on that, that you were a lot lot lot more forgiving towards her than she ever knew, let alone deserved.

Because that’s what it all came down to and became so brutally obvious on your daughter’s wedding day, sir, YOUR daughter’s wedding day + I saw how you reacted to seeing her mother in her dress that day + I still can’t unhear the crack in your voice when you told me how beautiful she looked + you tried to use those possessive pronouns on me—“YOUR WIFE” were the exact words you used, sir—to camouflage just how much it must have hurt to even begin to say something like that out loud in the very first place.

Seriously. There wasn’t a single day we didn't fight about you one way or another. She never ever ever got over you by getting anywhere near me, sir. I think it’s important you know that, for whatever it’s worth. It’s important to know where you stood then, is what I’m saying.

No one stood taller is what I’m saying.

I meant what I said when we were a stone’s throw from that massive barbecuer and it had already snowed at least once that morning: as soon as she even blinks that she wants any part of it/you back, I am stepping off for real + for good + that means forever.

I sincerely hope you are doing well,

Love,

T.

## BUT I’VE NOT TASTED ALL YOUR COOKING / WHO ARE YOU WHEN I’M NOT LOOKING?
-BLAKE, 2010.

I’d realized well before that night at Moda it would never be enough. The upper deck seats made her feel like she was falling the entire time, she said. 

“I mean, I *loved* it,” is what she actually said. 

The whole walkway smelled like cheap beer, which it did because it was beer but because it was Moda it wasn’t anywhere near cheap. The seats weren’t either. No, they weren’t closer to the stage where she would be able to see much better and fantasize better + also NO it wasn’t that she would rub one out with him in her mind later, it was that she would be seeing him thinking of another him, the one she always-always thought about no matter fucking what. I don’t know what I was thinking, buying those two tickets and getting her to the TRAIN STATION in time to make the opening acts. I don’t know what I was thinking because that was still the interstitial of Not Thinking At All For More Than A Picosecond. If only it had lasted that long. The interstitial. Not “us”. Bitch, please.

The next time we were together at Rose Quarter after that was the final time we were together period. I had left Portland at that point for good; she and her mother had the audacity to show up for graduation. Memorial Coliseum was where it would all end. MFUP, MMAB and I rode in via Highway 20, but of course, crossing over the bay bridge in Newport and taking that right with the sign that marks it from the very beginning and presumably to the very end: MEDAL OF HONOR HIGHWAY, ending in Boston, MA, thousands of miles east. Newport, it doesn’t get more west. And Boston, only they have ever been and it’s highly unlikely that I ever will.

MMAB doesn’t do well in lines and I’m not at all sure why. When we used to go to the Dollar Stores in Jerome, Oregon City, and Newport, MMAB always complained about Mexicans shopping in her stores and how just wished they would all stay home on Saturdays and why can’t they just learn English like everyone else. The Promissory Note MFUP and MMAB signed to sell their 10 acres in Jerome had other signatures with Spanish names, but since the entire form was printed in English already anyway, my parents signed it anyway and started taking payments from the bearers of those signatures with 6% interest tacked on top for the luxury of buying something like that—go figure—on payments. The very first time MMAB saw their property in South Beach, she scratched a check for the full asking price. The checkbook she used had her name tooled into the cover, misspelled even, with a “Y” at the end. And if nothing about MMAB was ever true, this sure as hell was, goddammit: MMAB never had a Y in her name a day in her fucking life.

I don’t know where MMAB is but MFUP is sticking right next to me because he gets anxious and weird anytime there are more than 4 people within 20 feet of him without cans or bottles or cups in their hands. By the time we’d walked up the ramps from the parking garage—twice—and to that big plaza between Moda and Memorial that floods before/after RQ events and spills onto Wiedler, near Broadway and just between the on/offramps for I-84. RQ is, for all intents and purposes, THE TRUE POINT OF BEGINNING of the very same freeway that shuttled me back and forth between Twin Falls and Featherville way more times than I could ever possibly remember if it weren’t for the fact that I was going up to the cabin before I could read, write, speak, THINK, FIGHT BACK.

But I’m not in fight mode that day. I’m trying to keep the peace. My ex-wife and also my then-wife both want me scalped and for pretty much the same reason: I’ve always been fucking lousy at following my programming. Full stop.

The crowd is thickening on the plaza, and the front doors are pretty far; you’d have to walk on top of shoulders, backs, heads to even think about getting there. 

The irony is I didn’t hear her voice first. The Siren, strangely silent now that I think about it when I heard her mother’s shrill and high Bumpkinese; to say the woman wore her area code as well as she did, well, that’s an understatement for sure. That pitch and that syntax and that vocab. Vintage Donna.

I must have been looking to the right when the electrons and protons finally reached my brain. That’s where MFUP was, standing just to my right. And why oh why do I have the bad feeling as I’m typing this that HE HEARD IT BEFORE I DID; hypervigilance isn’t a skill you can just forget or leave to dull and rust, you know. I have no idea if it runs in other families, but it sure as fuck runs in mine.

My head jerks right and then down and then I’m looking right at Donna, face to face, because all she has done is turned around. And before I can even think it—OH SHIT THAT MEANS SHE’S…—and that’s that moment I recognize all of it, and all of her. 

Her, standing right in front of me.

Me, standing right behind her.

Lining up and just getting through it like everyone else on the plaza that day. 

Singletons.

I got off two words total before she burst completely into her theatrical crying fit that MMAB came from across the somewhere else to console her and hug her and wish her the best life possible back in Idaho even though none of that had been decided yet. You usually know exactly where you stand with MMAB, I guess is what I’m saying. Their fake friendship shit had served their respective purposes and ran dry; all of it had, really, and there was no point other than trying to instigate something that could get transcribed later onto a police report. 

My two words were OH + SHIT, and uttered not so much from some kind of shock as a new strain of sobriety. I hadn’t paid a single bit of attention to where I was standing before I stood there. Pretty much like usual.

She ran away is what happened. She spun herself into a crying fit, MMAB went to console her and Donna popped off about something and the three Ice Queens held each other in check for I don’t know how many seconds before it all melted away, evaporated on all that shimmering concrete and hot, red rectangles of brick. Exit stage left. I don’t know if she expected me to chase her down and finish what was always just at best going to be an inadvertent exchange between us. The brush between us that never happened in the library at CSI when I started teaching there and she started studying there and we wouldn’t even know that part until way later anyway.

She ran away is what happened. That’s what I tell myself. I opened the door on her cage and she hopped right out as soon as she could and stopped faking the broken wing she was always claiming her never-ex husband gave her. 

She didn’t just run away is what happened, is what I’m saying.

Because she didn’t just run away. 

She FLEW.

And she was always self-admittedly bad with directions. “Not even with a compass,” she always said and grinned a toothy grin and maybe snorted a little snort. Her sister-in-law once put her on Facebook blast when they were fighting and I have never forgotten it. “That bitch ain’t just a little piggy cuz bitch is the whole fucking hog.”

## GUESS I’D RATHER HURT THAN FEEL NOTHING AT ALL.
-LADY A, 2009.
___________

There was a picosecond I thought it might happen. For all the wrong reasons it would have is what I’m saying; for all the right reasons it didn’t is also more what I’m saying.

Because I knew for more than just a picosecond that I’d fallen in love with you and didn’t know what to do. Because I knew why I had. And you did too and that’s why I said For All The Right Reasons.

But there was that more than just a picosecond. That’s me and I’m Earl and I’ve crash-landed back in TF and I wrote about all that already so I aint going back to the O.R. for it again; I at least decided THAT much in 2022. This is that night’s recording, by the way, whatever the fuck POINTS OF INTEREST is. I really thought when I started that recording I might die recording it; I didn’t know that I was about to start getting better and more than enough better to get myself out of the hospital bed that had reduced me to what it had reduced me to. 

I wrote all about that, too, already. Don’t fucking forget either, PINHEAD, that you did that. For almost two full years you went back through old pictures and made yourself process parts of that way sooner than you were ready to.

This though, this is all way before that. This though, SAM, this has nothing to do with any of the hospital stuff at all, other than I guess why I just had to stop a full play of U2’s cover of “Unchained Melody” from their gold-covered BEST OF album that was the clearest evidence of all they were waving the white flags on all the experiments that had produced something like ZOOTV and ACHTUNG BABY, but for U2, too, this is before all that ZOOTV stuff just like what I just said was what I just said. 

I didn’t think I was in love with you when I was in love with you. 

I didn’t think I was in love with you, simply and for no other reason than because I WAS in love with you.

I haven’t thought about this time of my life in any real depth or given it any real amount of space to run because I know I fell a lot harder than I thought I could/would/should. It surprised me. Scared me, too. It was entirely escapist to even daydream myself into the crushing stage with you, which I fought instantly whenever we shared any amount of space for any amount of time. By the time I was finishing my thesis project was also the same time I was giving up TF for good, which, in that moment, meant I was giving up on any possibility of staying behind and wasting my life waiting for something that clearly was never going to happen.

That sounds terrible and I don’t mean a single bit of it negatively. 

Because I’m still in love with you and always will be, because you calmed and brightened and occasionally held spaces with me when I really and truly was coming unraveled. It was 1997 and KLD was on trial and I was living back in TF and it all seems so obviously providential and has worked out about as okay as I guess it was ever going to be. You know, all things considered? 

I knew I was in love with you when I started crossing the canyon just to catch glimpse of what in some completely wormhole universe somewhere else entirely is the, yes, proverbial What Might Have Been. But I suppose all I ever really wanted to see was who you really were when you knew I wasn’t looking, when I wasn’t mapping your every facial gesture to every single time your mouth moved because I literally was hung up on not only what you said but oh my god THE WAY you said it, so light and snickerdoodle and, well, just so:

You.

I have only ever wished that you are as loved and cherished as you deserve. When I saw that our patriarchs passed on the same day, I recognized your cheeks and jawline; I also, of course, recognized that face and name and his wife’s name and how when he brought his brand new lipstick red VW New Beetle and parked it right out front so I could see it even though I worked all the way in the back, Jim Halpert-style, and you weren’t Pam-style because you just weren’t anymore a Pam than I was a Jim, but I honestly felt that one Saturday when you came through to do some work in your own office and we stayed away from each other the entire afternoon after sharing some pretty nervous Burger King rodeo burgers while we also devised ways to just stay away from each other for this very second and then do that again for the next second and then again for the next second and then just keep doing that until, well, I guess I’ll always just be sitting there in whatever chair you had in front of you of the two others in the room because you were the person people usually met in pairs and signed documents in pairs and left the room in pairs but not really necessarily the parking lot in pairs, let alone arrived in pairs or even, really, acknowledging any kind of pairing in the first place.

I meant it when I wrote it and then made sure it would surface eventually in print and that you would be able to see it someday for yourself if you ever ever ever really wondered?

Uh yeah. Oh god, yeah.

In a fucking heartbeat. 

I don’t know what I would have done that winter and spring without your kindnesses. I just really really don’t. That little blip of maybe 10 minutes tops of riding with you in your little emerald Honda Accord is something I will always be grateful for, to just have that space with you just long enough to feel that calm and know it to be real and know that it really was just long enough when you didn’t take the right to head towards Shoshone so you could linger a bit before you had to drive all the way back over the bridge, maybe grab some takeout—YO QUIERO TACO BELL!!—before crossing the county line, and then, oh fuck it, jump on the freeway and take that SECOND exit even though it’s the longer way home for you, but maybe when you slow to the T just past the offramp, maybe you’re still thinking about circling all the way back because why the fuck not and what would be the difference but by the time you get to those railroad tracks just past where they auction all the cattle like two and three times a week, and certainly by the time you get to Lincoln St and STILL don’t turn back towards the south, you’re convinced that river and that canyon and that bridge will keep how I feel about you and always will, ALL of this will keep me away from you?

Yeah. I mapped every single second as you drove those five short blocks, counting down all those numbered streets which don’t have numbers for names anymore. I probably secretly prayed you’d run the car out of gas but of course you didn’t; I probably secretly prayed you’d pretend you’d forgotten: you needed to run an errand first but hey it’s sort of on the way and you weren’t in a hurry anyway it didn’t sound like anyway, right but of course you didn’t; I probably and not so secretly said something about whatever song was either playing on your radio or whatever song I’d last heard on the Muzak in the office before we climbed into your car, always parked in the first spot just past the back security door because you were almost always one of the the first ones in the office, freeway construction permitting but of course. 

Somewhere in one of my library books is a single newspaper clipping that I will miss very much once I know for certain it is gone and I’ll never see it again. It’s one of those holiday ads that local businesses ran in The Times-News, the kind that regardless of what kind of business, showed you names and pictures of everyone who worked there, so as to give things that “home town” flavor if you will. There’s a portrait of you on that page that will always HAVE to be long enough, even though it isn’t. 

And it isn’t. Even though, of course, it is.

But make no mistake, then and forever. 

In a heartbeat.

The last time I ever saw you, you let me hold you. And I felt you hold me back. For that single beat, I felt you press into me and I hope you felt my entire chest fill with as much of that moment as my lungs would let me hold onto; I felt you let go so I did too; and there will always be at least one cell of me still holding my breath, forever set adrift in a memory bliss of
